INDIAN ELEPHANT

The Indian elephant (Elephas maximus indicus) is one of three recognized subspecies of the Asian elephant and native to mainland Asia. Since 1986, Elephas maximus has been listed as Endangered by the IUCN as the population has declined by at least 50% over the last three generations, estimated to be 60-75 years. The species is threatened by habitat loss, degradation and fragmentation.
